# 👔 HR Attrition Analysis Report

## Project Overview
This project investigates employee attrition patterns across a fictional company with 300 employees. The goal is to identify which departments, roles, and factors are most associated with employees leaving — helping HR teams take proactive steps to improve retention.

---

## 🎯 Key Questions Answered
- What is the overall attrition rate?
- Which departments have the highest turnover?
- Does job satisfaction affect attrition?
- Do salary levels influence whether employees leave?
- Does commute distance or work-life balance play a role?
- Which tenure group is most likely to leave?

## 📂 Dataset
- **Source:** Sample/simulated dataset
- **Records:** 300 employees
- **Fields:** Employee ID, Age, Gender, Department, Job Role, Years at Company, Monthly Salary, Job Satisfaction (1–4), Work Life Balance (1–4), Distance From Home, Attrition (Yes/No)

---

## 📈 Key Findings

### Attrition Rate
- Overall attrition rate calculated across all 300 employees
- Employees with **low job satisfaction (score 1)** had the highest attrition rates

### Department Insights
- Certain departments showed significantly higher turnover than others
- Sales and Operations tend to have higher attrition in most organizations

### Salary
- Employees who left had a **lower average salary** than those who stayed
- Low-salary employees (under $4,000/month) were more likely to leave

### Commute & Work-Life Balance
- Employees living **30+ km away** from the office showed higher attrition
- Poor work-life balance (score 1) strongly correlated with leaving

### Tenure
- Employees with **0–2 years** at the company were the most likely to leave
- Long-tenured employees (10+ years) showed the lowest attrition

## 💡 Recommendations
1. **Increase salaries** for low-paid roles — salary is a key driver of attrition
2. **Improve onboarding** for new employees (0–2 years) — highest risk group
3. **Offer remote/hybrid work** to employees with long commutes
4. **Run satisfaction surveys** regularly and act on low scores
5. **Focus retention efforts** on high-attrition departments
